Leo Edwards

London based Data Scientist leojpedwards@gmail.com

I am a London based data scientist, currently working on the Data Science Immersive course at General Assembly London.

I have a Master’s degree in Computer Science from UCL and an Undergraduate degree in Biological Science from Oxford University.

It was at Oxford that my interest in technology and data science really evolved as data analysis was covered in some depth to facilitate experiments into biological cell function. Learning to code in R for data analysis was an eye-opening experience and caused me to seek out more in the way of coding experience and data analysis projects.

Over the following summer I taught myself to code in Python and began projects to learn Machine Learning techniques as well as the bread and butter tools of Python data analytics.

After finishing my Master’s I had a strong desire to delve deeper into data science and to really challenge myself to turn my hobby into a career. This led me to the Data Science Immersive course at General Assembly. There I gained the experience and knowledge to approach data problems in a structured way to gain the most valuable insights.

My interests at the moment are largely in applying my knowledge of data and computer science into a data engineering or data science setting. In particular big data, machine learning, artificial intelligence and computational biology are my areas of interest and expertise.

I have a passion for combining my background in biology, computer science and data science. In particular in healthcare analytics which I feel is a vital and fascinating area of research to which I hope to contribute in the future.

Q and A

What is a book or movie that has been formative to your personality?

'The Beckoning Silence' by Joe Simpson

As a keen mountaineer I felt this book would appeal to me for the sense of adventure that it evoked. However as I read more the more it occurred to me how the lessons of the mountains could be applied to life more generally. Climbing has defined my personality in a big way by giving me a sense of perseverance, teamwork, constant challenge. These are qualities which I feel are encompassed not just in my climbing life but also my academic and soon my work life. I never back down from a challenge, whether a summit or a particularly complex technical problem. I seek out challenges as I find that is where I learn and improve. In the mountains teamwork is how a person survives, as a consequence I have developed strong collaborative skills.

What is a product, software or app that has made your life easier?

Quiver app

Quiver is an app that allows for note taking and organising. This app has helped me to organise my life for over a year now. Before I would have loose pages of notes all over my floor in barely comprehensible chaos. Now Quiver takes care of it and since I downloaded it I haven’t missed a deadline or lost a note. I can now happily describe myself as a well organised person whereas before it was a significant weakness for me.

What do you like to do when not coding?

Run

When I’m not coding I like to breathe the fresh air and run. I find it satisfying to experience the discipline that running requires, it helps my body and my mind to stay healthy. I like to compete against myself to see if I can be always improving. I also have found that the best way to solve a complex technical problem is to put on my running shoes and listen to a podcast unrelated to data or computing. This allows my subconscious to solve the problem (I call it productive laziness).
